Hannah Recht Data Analyst and Visualizer

I am a data nerd passionate about communicating complex, important information through visuals and statistics. I research, analyze, and visualize public policy data at the Urban Institute. Favorite tools include D3.js, R, and Leaflet.js. I ♥ open data.

I graduated from the University of Rochester with a bachelor's degree in mathematics and statistics, a minor in epidemiology, and a love of six month winters. When not doing all things data I play trumpet and watch any/all cooking competition shows.

Mapping the assistance gap

5.6 million people living in 21 states are in the assistance gap - uninsured prior to the ACA and earning too little for marketplace tax credits, but ineligible for Medicaid due to their state's non-expansion. Local estimates show the effects of varying state Medicaid eligibility thresholds.

Mapping the uninsured by race/ethnicity

View the geographic distribution of uninsurance by race/ethnicity under three scenarios: without the ACA, with the ACA, and with the ACA if all states expanded Medicaid. The full report additionally analyzes uninsurance for detailed Asian/Pacific Islander origin groups, Hispanic origin groups, and American Indian/Alaska Native tribes.
